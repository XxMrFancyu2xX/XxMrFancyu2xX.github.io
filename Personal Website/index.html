Merlon-Crenel Problem

Problem Description:

The Merlon-Crenel Problem involves finding all possible integer lengths for merlons \(M\) and crenels \(C\) on a castle wall of given length \(W\) with \( n \) merlons and \( n-1 \) crenels.

The lengths must satisfy the equation \( W = nM + (n-1)C \). The solution uses modular arithmetic to derive the constraints and provides the range for possible integer values of \( M \) and \( C \).

Applications of the Merlon-Crenel Problem

The Merlon-Crenel Problem can be applied in architectural design to determine feasible dimensions for castle battlements, ensuring structural integrity and aesthetic appeal. It also has applications in modular design and manufacturing processes where repetitive patterns and spacing are critical. Additionally, it can be used in digital design and game development for creating realistic medieval structures.
